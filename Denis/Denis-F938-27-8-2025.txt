The course methodology was conducted in a participatory approach with at least 80% of the course time dedicated to practical activities by participants. Before starting the training course contract agreement was made and signed between the Rufiji Environment Management Project, REMP (contracting authority) and the National Tree Seed Programme, NTSP (contractor) Twenty participants (10 females and 10 males) were nominated by the village governments of the 4 selected villages: Mtanza msona, Mbunju Mvuleni, Twasalie and Taja. REMP supplied camping facilities, meals, transport, stationers and some training equipment. The following activities were carried out by the contractor:   Training notes were prepared and binded in the form of a booklet (76 pages) They were written in simple Kiswahili and well illustrated by line drawings. Each participant was given a copy at the beginning of the course.   A detailed day-by-day syllabus was prepared for 10 day course (See Appendix  1, Annex 5).   A day-by-day timetable was prepared for the 10-day course (See Appendix  2). Each participant was given a copy at the beginning of the course. This was followed as much as possible through the course period.   Materials such earth balls, banana fibre pots, bamboo pots, seeds, botanical specimens etc. were brought to the venue for demonstration purposes.   Suitable propagation and regeneration sites were selected at Bizi Hamlet south of river Rufiji at Mtanza Msona. Participants were trained through discussions on criteria used to select this site.   Demonstrations and practicals on subjects listed in the syllabus were based on low input methods affordable by individuals and communities.   Kiswahili was used as the main language of training but also scientific and Kindengereko languages were used where necessary, especially for plant names.   At the end of the course certificates were issued all 20 participants who successfully completed the training course. (See Appendix  4).   A participatory evaluation of the course was conducted (See Appendix  3)   Recommendations for improvement of future courses included:  Observation: This course was conducted in a participatory approach in which practicals took 80% and theory 20% of the course time. This was well appreciated by participants. Recommendation: This approach should continue to be used in coming courses: iii  Observation: The duration of the course was only 10 days. This period was somehow short considering the subjects which were covered. During evaluation majority of the participants indicated that the period was short.  REMP Technical Report 23: Tree Seeds Workshop Recommendation: We recommended that next time when there is a similar course the duration should be 14 days.  Observation: Twenty participants for the whole Rufiji District are not enough to teach others and cause impact on tree planting activities. Recommendation: Another course like this with another 20 participants should be organised at the same venue in June    Observation: Through discussions among participants and facilitators it was observed that participants are too localised. They have not had the chance of visiting other villages in Tanzania where they could have learnt on how other people handle tree seeds and raise seedlings. Recommendation: REMP should organise a study tour for the 20 participants who attended this course. Recommended places to visit include:   National Tree Seed Programme (NTSP), Morogoro   Horticulture Unit of SUA, Morogoro   Dodoma Environment Network (DONET), Dodoma   Makini Women Group, Dodoma   Handeni intergrated Agroforestry Project (HIAD), Handeni.   Kilimanjaro Village Afforestation Project, Same   Northern Zone Mangrove Project, Tanga  Observation: REMP staff may not be aware on how participants will apply the knowledge they obtained. Recommendation: REMP staff (Field Officers) should make a follow up by visiting and advise participants in their villages. This may be done at an interval of 6 months starting form November    Observation: Participants were provided with almost everything for their subsistence during the course period. Nevertheless they complained of not being paid allowance for incidentals.
