Mbeya ina mtandao mzuri wa mawasiliano kwani barabara kubwa zinapitamkoani na kuunganisha bandari za Dar es Salaam na Mombasa pamoja na Zambiana Malawi na nchi nyingine za Afrika kupitia barabara na reli na kutengeneza upenyowenye mchanganyiko wa watu kati ya nchi hizi tatu na zingine za kusini.Lengo kuu la timu iliyotafiti na kukusanya habari kuhusu hali ya UKIMWIya mkoa wa Mbeya lilikuwa ni kufanya ufuatiliaji na tathmini ya hali ya UKIMWI yaMkoa wa Mbeya. Huu ni utaratibu wa kawaida wa mara kwa mara wa TACAIDS wakutembelea Mkoa ili kupata ufahamu bora zaidi kuhusu afua zinazohusiana na UKIMWIkatika Mkoa. Ratiba ilikuwa ni yenye kueleweka sana na ilitengenezwa maalum kwaajili ya kukutanisha timu na vikundi tofauti vya watu wakiwemo mashirika ya kidini,wasichana na wanaume, wanawake, watu waishio na virusi vya UKIMWI, vikundimaalumu vya walengwa kama wahudumu wa baa na sekta za serikali kama elimu,kilimo na maendeleo ya jamii. Leo UKIMWI unatambulika si tu kama jambo kubwa lakiafya peke yake, bali pia kama tatizo la kijamii na kimaendeleo katika nchi, mkoa, wilayana jamii kwa ujumla. Ilipokuwa mkoa wa Mbeya timu ilitembelea wilaya nne kati yawilaya nane zilizopo mkoani humo. Wilaya zilizotembelewa zilichaguliwa na mamlakaza mkoa kwa kushirikiana na mamlaka za wilaya.TACAIDS walitayarisha orodha ya maswali zilizosambazwa kwa vikundimbalimbali kabla ya kutembelea vikundi hivyo kwa ajili ya kufanya majadiliano. Timuya TACAIDS iligawanywa katika timu mbalimbali zilizotembelea vikundi tofauti kamavile wanawake, vijana, watu waishio na virusi vya UKIMWI, (wazee, wahudumu wa baana wafanyakazi wa nyumba za wageni, mashirika ya kidini, taasisi za elimu, vituo vyautafiti na mashirika mateule makuu ya wakala wa uwezeshaji wa mkoa.Timu ilitembelea wilaya za Kyela, Chunya, Mbarali na Mbozi. Katika wilaya hiziratiba ya ziara iliandaliwa na Mratibu wa Ukimwi wa Halmashauri wa Wilaya na vikundimbalimbali zikiwemo asasi zisizo za kiserikali na maafisa wa serikali kama vile wakuuwa idara za kilimo, elimu na utawala za wilaya. Region covers a surface area of 64,000 square kilometers and has apopulation of 2,070,046 (990,825 men and 1,079,221 women). The child bearing age (15-49 years) population is 423,945. The HIV prevalence rate of Mbeya is , the highest inthe country (the national HIV prevalence rate is 7 %). Mbeya has a good communicationnetwork as highways cross the region connecting the harbors of Dar es Salaam andMombasa with Zambia and Malawi together with other African countries through roadand railways creating a corridor of social interaction between the three countries and otherin the South.The main objective of the Mbeya Mission Team was to conduct a monitoring andevaluation of the HIV/AIDS situation in Mbeya region. This was a routine mission of TACAIDSto visit the region for a better understanding of the interventions related to HIV/AIDS in theregion. The timetable was very comprehensive and designed for the team to meet differentgroups of people including faith based organizations, girls and boys, women, people livingwith HIV/AIDS, specific target groups such as barmaids and government sectors likeeducation, agriculture and community development. Today HIV/AIDS is recognized notonly as a major public health concern, but also a social and development problem in thecountry, region, district and community at large. While in Mbeya region the team visited fourdistricts out of eight in the region. The districts visited were selected by regional authoritiesin consultation with the district authorities.TACAIDS had prepared questionnaire forms that were distributed to various groupsbefore visiting these respective groups for discussions. The TACAIDS team was divided intoteams which visited different groups such as women, young people, people living withHIV/AIDS, elderly people, bar workers and guest house attendants, faith based institutions,educational institutions, CTC, research centers and key selected RFA organizations.The team visited the districts of Kyela, Chunya, Mbarali and Mbozi. In these districtsthe visiting schedule was planned by CHAC and several groups including NGOs andgovernment officials such as district heads of the departments of agriculture, education andadministration. Heads of primary schools, ward counselors and faith based organizationssuch as BAKWATA were also involved in the deliberations.The methodology used was participatory whereby all groups were involvedduring discussion. The aim of the discussion was to enlighten the TACAIDS mission on theimplementation of regional HIV/AIDS activities, getting suggestions on what the discussantsthink should be done in order to strengthen HIV/AIDS programmes in the region.The consultation meetings helped TACAIDS to have an advantage by creatingawareness, advocating and sensitizing the community groups to understand that the waragainst HIV/AIDS is for them and not for anyone else. Such kinds of motives motivate thestakeholders at regional and district level to keep on fighting this scourge disease.The advantages of this methodology was also to provide technical support on fundraising modalities and technical issues such as funding procedures and proper settlementof funds where it was identified during the discussion that to some recipients there was aproblem especially in knowledge of the procedures.ASSESSMENT TEAMThe Mbeya Mission Report was prepared by TACAIDS, which sent a team to Mbeyato conduct a monitoring and evaluation of the HIV/AIDS situation in Mbeya region. Theteam comprised of TACAIDS staff, NACOPHA representatives, Ministry of Health and SocialWelfare staff, Regional and District Health authorities, Council HIV and AIDS coordinators,GTZ (the Regional Facilitating Agency) staff, development partners from the World Bank andjournalists.TACAIDSHon. In case there is an NGO which gets fundsfrom another source, the NGO without support should be given a priority.n In any donor funded HIV/AIDS intervention, an exit strategy should developed so thatafter the donor support ends the interventions cannot be stopped prematurely.n It is recommended that, there is a wealth of HIV/AIDS experiences at communitylevel which are not documented. Therefore, there is a need to develop a culture ofdocumenting the vast experiences existing in the field, rather than waiting for externalpeople to document and own the work.n The people who are working in High transmission areas are a group at high risk tobe infected with HIV. Thus, for target groups like barmaids, local brew sellers, hotelattendants and girls who are moving from one bar to another hunting for men specialprogrammes should be developed to rescue this target group, who by implication areconsidered to be sex workers.n Since there is a scarcity of IEC materials on HIV/AIDS in the country, of programmeswhich have are prepared and produced, the materials should be contacted for thepossibility of reproducing more and selling to other institutions. An inventory needs tobe done in order to know what is existing and where the materials can be obtained andthe result of the inventory be communicated to different institutions. The institutionscan be requested to order on time so that they can be reprinted according to demand.n The barmaids should form group in order to create solidarity in the prevention of HIV/AIDSn HIV/AIDS intervention should focus the children and the need to be open during theirHIV/AIDS sessions is essentialn Cross border HIV/AIDS intervention should considered with the involvement of differentactors including religious leaders.n Provision of HIV/AIDS education on condom technology for religious leadersn To promote and strengthen best practicesn To continue with vaccine preparationn To continue with mapping and assessment of all CSO and wards to implementCommunity Partnership Plan (CPP).n Strengthen the existing interventions as they have proved to be working throughreduction of STI and HIV prevalence.n Scale up PE in HTA, P/schools and Work places including health sectorn Strengthen data collection especially AIDS cases and reporting system through closesupportive supervision.n To have more community based response involving strong government leadership andCSOs through stable and functioning CMAC,n To have more community sensitization on ART using different implementers andstakeholders.n To have more staff trained and sensitized on ARTn Strengthen preparation of the future vaccine.n To develop cross-border programs in partnership with neighboring countries (Malawi,Zambia and Democratic Republic of Congo) HIV and AIDS initiatives. As noted, there isvery high interaction of people from Malawi, Zambia and Democratic Republic of Congoat border sites, and was reported as the higher transmission driver of HIV epidemic inthe regionn To provide information, and to reach out and touch, TACAIDS and other stakeholdersshould continue to facilitate documentation and sharing of lessons learnt from HIV andAIDS interventions.CONCLUSIONAs we still have a large population being free from HIV/AIDS infection, prevention has tobe given priority as the first pillar. Each individual must be empowered through educationto make informed decisions about their behavior as many might be acting in ignorance.However the ARVT as an additional resource, which does not replace prevention, has to begiven its due weight but carefully so as to avoid complacence about HIV transmission.
